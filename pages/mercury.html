Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun.
due to its proximity it is not easily seen except during twilight.
For every two orbits of the Sun, Mercury completes three rotations about its axis.
Mercury can be observed from the Earth passing across the face of the Sun,
in an event called a transit, the next will occur on the 9th May 2016.


About Mercury

  • Mass: 3.285×10^23 kg (0.055 x Earth)
  • Equatorial Diameter: 4,879
  • Equatorial Circumference: 15,329 km
  • Moons: none
  • Orbit Distance: 57,909,227 km (0.39 AU)
  • Orbit Period: 87.97 Earth days
  • Surface Temperature: -173 to 427°C

Top Facts

  1. Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System
  2. Mercury is the second densest planet
  3. Mercury has a molten iron core
